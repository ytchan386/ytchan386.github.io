JPG/JPEG (Joint Photographic Experts Group) is a popular image format noted for its ability to compress digital photos without drastically reducing their quality. JPG files are frequently used for picture sharing online or in digital publications since they work with a variety of hardware and software programs. JPG files are widely used for photographs, digital artwork, and other sorts of images requiring fine detail and colour fidelity. The format analyses the colour and brightness of nearby pixels before arranging them into blocks that can be compressed without losing too much detail.


What is a JPG/JPEG?

  • Name: Joint Photographic Experts Group, in short JPG or JPEG
  • Invented in 1986, by a group of experts from the International Organization for Standardization (ISO) and the International Electrotechnical Commission (IEC).
  • Designed to allow for the efficient compression of digital images without significant loss of quality.
  • Today, the JPG format is one of the most widely used image formats in the world.

    How is JPG better than the other image formats?

  • File size: When compared to other picture formats like BMP or TIFF, JPG images can be compressed without significantly losing detail, making them lower in size. This makes it simpler to upload, share, and save them online.
  • Compatibility: JPG files are compatible with a wide range of devices and software applications, making them a versatile choice for sharing images. JPG is typically the default image format used by digital cameras and smartphones.
  • Quality: JPG files are capable of producing high-quality images with a wide range of colours and tones, making them a popular choice for photographers and graphic designers.
  • Editing: JPG files can be edited with a large number of software applications, allowing for adjustments to brightness, contrast, and other image attributes.

    • How is JPG worse than the other image formats?

  • Lossy Compression: Lossy Compression: JPG uses lossy compression, which results in a small amount of detail being lost during compression. Image quality may suffer as a result, especially if the compression setting is set too high.
  • Limited Transparency: JPG does not support transparency, which means that photos with transparent backgrounds cannot be stored in this format. Other image formats like PNG and GIF support transparency.
  • Limited Editing: JPG is a compressed image format, editing JPG files is more challenging than editing BMP or TIFF files. Image quality might be lost through repeated editing.
  • Limited Colour Depth: JPG has 24-bit colour depth, which may not be sufficient for images that require a higher level of colour accuracy or detail.
  • Artefacts: JPG compression can occasionally produce visual artefacts like blockiness or blurriness, especially in areas with strong contrast or sharp edges.

    How to create JPG?

    A JPG image can be made using a number of techniques and software tools, and the process is simple and straightforward.

    1. Using a Digital Camera or Smartphone: JPG format is typically the default image format for digital cameras and smartphones. Just use the camera on your device to capture a photo, and the resulting file will be stored as a JPG image.
    2. Using Image Editing Software: Most image editing software, such as Adobe Photoshop, Adobe Lightroom, or DxO PhotoLab, allow you to create and save images in JPEG format. Create or modify an image with the app, then pick "Save As" or "Export" and choose JPG as the file type.
    3. Quality: JPG files are capable of producing high-quality images with a range of colours and tones, making them a popular choice for photographers and graphic designers.
    4. Using Online Converters: There are many online image converters that allow you to convert images to JPG format. Simply upload your image to the converter and select JPG as the output format. Some popular online converters include Online-Convert, Zamzar, and Convertio.

    How to read JPG files on a desktop?

    1. For Windows
      Open File Explorer by clicking on the folder icon on the taskbar or by pressing the Windows key + E. Double-click on the JPEG file to open it in Windows Photos, which is the default image viewer in Windows 10.
    2. For Mac
      Open Finder by clicking on the Finder icon on the Dock or by pressing Command + Spacebar and typing "Finder". Double-click on the file to open it in Preview, which is the default image viewer in macOS.
